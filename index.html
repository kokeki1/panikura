Slang Dictionary

This page documents common slang, jargon, and terminology used within the Panikura group. This section is intended to clarify inside jokes, shorthand, and frequently used expressions.

blorbified
An emotional attachment to something (often a fictional character or object) that is disproportionate or irrational.
Example: "I just met this frog NPC and I’m already blorbified."
egg mode
A humorous term for a state of being confused, mentally checked-out, or functionally useless.
Example: "I entered egg mode five minutes into the Zoom call."
panikura
A flexible term used as a verb, noun, or exclamation within the group. Often context-specific and intentionally ambiguous.
Example: "Don’t panikura over it." / "That was such a panikura moment."
grumbo
Used to describe someone acting foolish, grumpy, or both. Can be affectionate or mildly insulting depending on context.
Example: "Stop being a grumbo and just join the call."
brain goo
Describes cognitive fatigue, mental burnout, or lack of focus.
Example: "After writing that essay, my brain was literal goo."
clown-certified
Acknowledged as a mistake or embarrassing moment, but accepted or laughed off by the group.
Example: "You told your crush about our DnD campaign? Clown-certified."
emoji speak
Spoken communication that mimics emojis or internet shorthand.
Example: "When she said 'skull emoji' out loud, I couldn’t stop laughing."
